New England Daily Snow

Snow and rain

By Meteorologist Jay Cordeira
Friday March 31, 2023 10:17am EDT

View This Post on OpenSnow

Summary

A complex storm track develops over New England with three rounds of precipitation on Friday (wet snow), Saturday (rain), and Saturday night (rain, possibly thunder). Warmer weather returns next week.

Short Term Forecast

NAM model forecast animation for 8AM Friday through 2AM Sunday. 

An interesting three-part storm is on tap for the weekend with both snow and rain likely across the region. Sunshine will wane quickly on Friday with snow arriving Friday evening for locations in New Hampshire and Maine. This first part of the storm is likely going to be too warm in Vermont, especially for locations in southern Vermont and New Hampshire to produce much if any snow. Locations in northern Vermont, the Whites, and much of Maine will remain cold enough to see a couple inches of snowfall on Friday night.

National Weather Service snowfall maps through Sunday.

The second part of the storm arrives on Saturday morning and will be mostly rain and rain showers across the region. Much of the precipitation punches through the region by lunchtime and clearing, especially south, in the warm sector of the storm is going to result in some pretty warm temperatures. Saturday afternoon could be pretty nice across central and southern New England.

The last part of the storm is Saturday night with one final round of showers and possibly thunderstorms. All in all, not really a washout but could be pretty wet with >1" of rain is possible in and around the North Country. The recent cold snap helped stabilize the snowpack, so I'm optimistic we won't see too much melt. Although, ponding could be an issue. 

Extended Forecast

The extended range forecast has all the feels of Spring with warmer temperature creeping through the 40s next week on the slopes with 50s likely across the North Country Valleys. Spring skiing will be in full force, but careful for mid-week closures on many resorts as we roll into April. Most resorts will start closing or transition to final weekend or weekend-only operations. 

ECMWF ensemble grid for 24-hour snowfall through mid April in Central Vermont.

Winter isn't completely over at least according the ECMWF ensemble. The model is predicting at least another shot at some wintry weather next weekend (9-10 April) with 50/50 odds of at least some snow. The storm track seems to favor at least something developing along the coast or, more likely, out to sea. While not necessarily a lock for snow, it does suggest that the warmth may be cut back for a couple days into next weekend with a chance of precipitation. 

-Dr. Jay
