New England Daily Snow

Weekend Update

By Meteorologist Jay Cordeira
Sunday April 2, 2023 8:00am EDT

View This Post on OpenSnow

National Weather Service reports mostly on 1 April (Saturday).

Snowfall reports on Saturday ranged from 2-5" across the region with highest amounts across Central New Hampshire and into Maine. Much of this snow fell early on Friday night with a transition to rain by Saturday morning. The transition to rain and warming temperatures led to much of this snow to turn to glop and melt through Saturday afternoon.

Temperatures have dropped back below freezing across the North Country here by Sunday morning with pretty windy conditions as of 8:00AM. We've seen wind gusts >40 mph in many valleys with gusts near 100 mph on the summit of Mt. Washington. Although windy, temperatures should settle into the 30-40F range with increasing sunshine for Sunday. Snow conditions should also improve over yesterday's soft surfaces.

GFS model forecast animation for 2PM Monday through 2PM Tuesday. 

Our next shot at precipitation arrives quickly on Monday night as high pressure slides offshore and a weak cold front sags across the region. Precipitation looks like it will most likely be light rain with a transition to light snow possible at elevation on the backside into Tuesday. 

GFS model forecast animation for 2AM Wednesday through 8PM Thursday.

Warmer air floods into the region by the end of the week with more rain expected on Wednesday night and Thursday. There is a chance, as suggested by the GFS model, that precipitation could start out as sleet across the Whites and in Western Maine, but overall the temperatures are likely going to be just too warm to support snow out of this system.

Extended range temperature and precipitation outlook for 9-15 April from the Climate Prediction Center.

Looking out into next week, the Climate Prediction Center is predicting near normal precipitation trends with slightly below normal temperatures for 9-15 April. Normal this time of year is above freezing, so cooler temperatures keeps the snowpack from melting too quickly, which is good for extending seasons though mid-month at many resorts and a few, like Killington, that will be skiing into May. 

-Dr. Jay
