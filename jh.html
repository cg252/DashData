Jackson Hole Daily Snow

Heavy snow and strong winds this weekend

By Meteorologist Alan Smith
Friday March 31, 2023 9:27am MDT

View This Post on OpenSnow

Summary

A moist westerly flow in between storm systems will result in snow showers across the Tetons on Friday with several more inches of snow expected. A strong storm will then impact the area Sat-Sat night with heavy snow and strong winds. Snowfall rates will decrease Sun but winds will remain strong. Additional snow showers are expected Mon-Wed on the back edge of a storm impacting Central/Eastern WY.

Short Term Forecast

Snow Totals:

Snowfall on Thursday-Thursday night ranged from 3-5 inches above 8,500 feet and a trace to an inch across the lower elevations. The west side of the Tetons did a little better than the east side with Targhee reporting 5 inches.

Here are the latest 2-day snow totals as of Friday AM:

  • 8" - Grand Targhee Chief Joseph Bowl
  • 7" - Surprise Meadow
  • 6" - Jackson Hole Rendezvous Bowl
  • 6" - Jackson Hole Raymer Plot
  • 3" - Snow King
  • 3" - Togwotee Pass
  • 3" - Driggs
  • 1" - Jackson Hole Mid-Mountain
  • 1" - Jackson

The Rendezvous Bowl Plot will finish the month of March with 117 inches of snow, making this the 4th snowiest March on record dating back to 1975. Grand Targhee's Chief Joseph Bowl Plots has reached 127 inches, making this the snowiest in the last 13 years (records only date back to 2011).

For total snowfall through April 1st, the Rendezvous Bowl Plot is now in 3rd place with 558 inches and will likely move into second place with one more day to go.

Forecast for Friday to Friday Night:

Snow showers will continue throughout the day on Friday as we remain in a consistent moist westerly flow. Snowfall will largely be orographic/terrain-driven in nature, meaning the higher elevations will see the most consistent snowfall with lighter and more intermittent snow across the lower elevations. 

Winds aloft will be out of the west/northwest from Friday morning through early afternoon, which will favor Grand Targhee and the west side of the Tetons the most, before veering to west/southwest from late Friday afternoon through Friday night.

Ridgetop wind speeds will increase on Friday afternoon to 10-20 mph with gusts to 25-35 mph. Winds will also gust to 20-25 mph in the valley. A gradual increase in ridgetop winds out of the west/southwest can also be expected on Friday night with speeds of 15-25 mph and gusts of 30-40 mph.

Here is my snow forecast for Friday AM through Saturday AM:

  • 4-8" - Grand Targhee
  • 3-7" - Jackson Hole above 8,000 feet
  • 1-2" - Snow King
  • TR-1" - Jackson Hole Valley
  • TR-1" - Teton Valley

Cold temperatures aloft will result in dry & powdery snow quality across the higher elevations with snow-liquid ratios of around 15:1.

High temperatures at 9,000 feet will reach the low 20s on Friday with overnight lows in the mid teens. Highs in the valleys will reach the mid/upper 30s on Friday with overnight lows in the upper 20s to low 30s.

Forecast for Saturday to Saturday Night:

A strong storm will impact the area during this period. A strong jet stream will be nosing its way into Northwest Wyoming along with an abundance of moisture and energy. Since my last post, the jet has trended a little further south and into a better position to enhance snowfall rates across the Tetons.

Snow showers will ongoing through Saturday morning, then snowfall will become heavy and more widespread from Saturday afternoon through Saturday night. 

Here is my snow forecast for Saturday AM through Sunday AM:

  • 6-12" - Jackson Hole above 8,000 feet
  • 6-12" - Grand Targhee
  • 3-6" - Snow King
  • 1-3" - Jackson Hole Valley
  • 1-3" - Teton Valley

Most if not all of the valley accumulations will occur on Saturday night/early Sunday morning once temperatures fall below freezing and we lose solar radiation.

In addition to heavy snow, strong west/southwest winds will also be a factor in this storm. On Saturday morning ridgetop winds of 20-30 mph can be expected with gusts to 40-50 mph. On Saturday afternoon and Saturday night, winds will increase to 25-40 mph with gusts to 60-70 mph. Lift operations may very well be impacted on Saturday afternoon. 

Valley winds will also be strong out of the southwest during this event. On Saturday morning, winds of 15-20 mph with gusts to 25-30 mph can be expected. On Saturday afternoon and Saturday night, winds will increase to 20-30 mph with gusts of 40-50 mph possible.

This snow event will be slightly higher-density compared to Friday, but we should still end up with snow-liquid ratios of around 13:1 which is medium to relatively low density for this time of year. 

High temperatures at 9,000 feet will reach the low/mid 20s on Saturday with overnight lows falling into the low teens as a cold front moves through late Saturday night. Highs in the valleys will top out in the upper 30s to near 40 early Saturday afternoon with overnight lows in the upper 20s.

Forecast for Sunday to Sunday Night:

The storm system will start to deepen further south across the Great Basin on Sunday and while we will have plenty of lingering moisture to work with, snowfall rates are trending lighter for the backside of this storm behind Saturday night's cold front. 

Light to moderate snow will still continue throughout the day on Sunday and winds will remain strong as well, though not quite as strong as Saturday. Snow will then taper off to flurries on Sunday night with a relative lull in the pattern expected.

Here is my snow forecast for Sunday AM through Monday AM:

  • 3-5" - Jackson Hole above 8,000 feet
  • 2-4" - Grand Targhee
  • 1-3" - Snow King
  • TR-1" - Jackson Hole Valley
  • TR-1" - Teton Valley

Strong west/southwest winds will remain a factor on Sunday and could still potentially impact lift operations. Ridgetop winds of 20-30 mph with gusts to 45-55 mph can be expected throughout the day on Sunday, while the valleys will see winds of 15-25 mph with gusts to 30-40 mph.

Sunday's backside snowfall will be lower density (15:1 ratios) as a colder airmass takes hold behind Saturday night's cold front. High temperatures at 9,000 feet will reach the mid teens on Sunday with overnight lows near 10 on Sunday night. Highs will reach the mid 30s in the valleys with lows in the mid teens on Sunday night.

3-Day Snow Totals from Friday through Sunday:

Total snowfall during this period will generally range from 1-2 feet in the Tetons, 5-11 inches at Snow King, and 1-5 inches in the valleys. Keep in mind that melting will also occur in the valleys during the afternoon hours.

Skiing Conditions:

Snow adding up over multiple days will result in deep conditions on the slopes this weekend, but strong winds will likely impact lift operations at times – especially across the higher elevations where the deepest snow is expected.

For backcountry skiers, the high winds combined with new snow will also result in elevated avalanche danger.

Mid to low elevations will feature variable and in many cases crusty conditions due to lighter snowfall and warmer temperatures, not to mention strong winds.

Even though snowfall is expected to be minimal on Sunday night, Monday morning should still be a good time to get out and enjoy the fresh powder once winds have calmed down.

Travel Conditions:

Winter conditions will primarily be confined to Teton Pass and Togwotee Pass on Friday, save for some minor slush on the valley roads early on Friday. Road surfaces over the pass will feature a mix of snowpacked, slushy, and icy conditions with some melting possible in the afternoons.

Travel conditions will worsen over the weekend as the wind picks up while periods of moderate to heavy snow can also be expected. Blowing snow can be expected at times over the pass with the worst conditions expected from Saturday afternoon through Sunday afternoon (Saturday night/Sunday AM will be worst of all). Be prepared for potential road closures due to strong winds and/or accidents. 

Slushy accumulations will be possible on the valley roads on Sunday morning with good pavement melting conditions expected on Saturday afternoon and Sunday. However, strong winds on both Saturday and Sunday will result in areas of blowing snow and drifting onto the roads in open/exposed areas. Sunday morning will be the highest impact travel period in the valleys.

Extended Forecast

Outlook for Monday (April 3) to Tuesday (April 4):

The storm system will move across the Central Rockies and deepen into a closed low pressure system (winds blowing counterclockwise all the way around a center of low pressure) on Monday before slowing down as the center of the low moves into Northeast Colorado.

Strong southerly and easterly winds around the low will transport moisture into Central and Eastern Wyoming where heavy snow and potential blizzard conditions can be expected.

On the backside of this storm, we will see varying amounts of moisture reaching the Tetons from Monday afternoon through Tuesday night. But it's a messy setup as winds look highly variable. At times, we will likely see northeast or southeast winds which are not favorable for Teton snow, but we could also see southwest or northwest winds at times which would be more favorable. 

Bottom line, this is a tricky setup for the Tetons but generally not a pattern that is conducive to heavy snow. For now, I would expect anywhere from a dusting to a few inches on both Monday and Tuesday across the Tetons with light accumulations also possible in the valleys. 

Outlook for Wednesday (April 5) to Thursday (April 6):

We could see enough lingering moisture for occasional flurries, especially on Wednesday, but unseasonably cold air behind a cold front on Tuesday will be the big story during this period.

While we are still the better part of a week away, highs are currently projected to be in the mid teens at 9,000 feet and near freezing in the valleys both days. Depending on cloud cover, we could potentially drop to near zero in the valleys, which would put us in record territory for this time of year.

Outlook for April 7th-14th:

Confidence is increasing that we will finally see some hints of spring during this period. A ridge of high pressure is projected to build over the Western U.S. during this period, resulting in dry conditions and more abundant sunshine through at least closing weekend at Jackson Hole (April 8th-9th) and likely into the start of the April 10th week as well.

Temperatures will start out well below average early in this period, but will warm up as time goes on and we should eventually approach more seasonal levels for this time of year (average highs in Jackson in early/mid April are in the low 50s).

Thanks so much for reading! Next update on Saturday (April 1).

ALAN SMITH
